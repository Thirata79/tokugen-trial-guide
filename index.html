Trial Booking — Operator Guide

TOKUGEN DOJO · KARATE IN ENGLISH
For Rob & family · how to handle online trial requests

What this is. People can now book a free trial lesson online — either inside LINE or on the website. When someone books, you decide to Confirm or Decline, and the system automatically messages the customer at every step. Nothing is ever confirmed until you confirm it.

How you're alerted depends on how they booked: LINE bookings ping you in the LINE chat with buttons (fastest); website bookings email you at info@tokugendojo.com with a link to the admin page. Either way, the admin page gives you full control. This guide covers both.

1How a booking flows

① Customer books — picks a class, one date & time, and enters their name + email.
▼
② You're alerted — booked in LINE? a “🆕 New trial request” message with buttons. Booked on the website? an email to info@tokugendojo.com with their details and a link to the admin page.
▼
③ You Confirm or Decline — one tap in LINE, or from the admin page.
▼
④ The customer is messaged automatically — confirmation + access details, or a polite “let’s find another date” — sent in LINE or by email, matching how they booked.
⚠️ A new request is only held, not booked. The time slot is reserved so no one else can grab it, but the customer is told “not confirmed yet.” It becomes a real booking the moment you Confirm.

3Way A — From the LINE alert LINE bookings

When someone books inside LINE, you’ll see a message like “🆕 New trial request — name · class · requested time” with these buttons:

Confirm Decline Open admin page

Confirm — books the lesson and automatically sends the customer their confirmation + access details.
Decline — automatically sends the customer a polite note that the requested date isn’t available (generic reason).
Open admin page — opens the full web page (Way B) — use this when you want to type a specific reason, cancel, or review details.

After you tap, you get a short confirmation back, e.g. “✅ Confirmed (Name · date).”

🌐 Website bookings don’t have these LINE buttons. They arrive as an email at info@tokugendojo.com — open the admin-page link in that email and Confirm/Decline there (Way B, below).
If two of you tap on the same request, whoever taps first wins. The second person simply sees “This request was already handled.” No harm done.

Open the link, enter the password, and you’re in. Top-right has a language switch — tap EN for English. This is also where you handle website (email) bookings — the link in the alert email brings you straight here.

What you’ll see

A list of requests (Pending + Confirmed). Each row shows: the requested date & time, the class, the participant (name, guardian, how they heard about us), and contact (email, and whether they’re linked on LINE).

What you can do on each request

ButtonWhat it does

DeclineYou type a reason; the customer receives it in their own language (Japanese or English).

5What the customer receives automatically

Each message goes out through the same channel the customer used: people who booked in LINE get a LINE message; people who booked on the website get an email. Same content either way.

When…The customer is sent…
They book“Request received — not confirmed yet. The dojo will get back to you.”
You ConfirmConfirmation with the date/time, the address, what to bring, and when to arrive.
You DeclineA polite note that the requested date isn’t available, inviting them to choose another.
You CancelAn apology that the confirmed lesson had to be cancelled.

Tip: you can always preview these with the ✉ Message button on the admin page before you act.

6Good to know

  • Only name + email are required from the customer. Phone number and age are collected later, at enrollment — not at the trial stage.
  • The class tells you the age group — Junior (4–7), Youth (8–14), Adults (15+) — so you don’t need to ask the child’s age.
  • One date per request. The customer picks a single preferred date & time.
  • No double-booking. Once a slot is confirmed, it’s blocked for everyone else.
  • Two front doors, one back office. LINE and website bookings both land in the same admin list and the same calendar — nothing extra to manage.
  • “How did you hear about us?” is shown on each request (Google / social media / referral / flyer / other) — useful for knowing what’s working.
